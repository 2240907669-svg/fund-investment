# Event-Driven Analysis and Teaching Contract

Use this reference when a news event or market shock is part of a fund decision.

## Catalyst workflow

1. **Verify the fact**: use primary or authoritative sources; record event time, publication time, source, and exactly what is new.
2. **Measure surprise**: distinguish a genuinely unexpected result from a scheduled or widely anticipated milestone. A positive event can still produce a price decline when it was already priced in.
3. **Align the clock**: place the event on the trading timeline. Separate moves before the event, the immediate reaction, the close, and the next session. Do not explain an earlier move with a later headline.
4. **Seek market confirmation**: check the affected sector against the broad market using return, relative strength, turnover, advance/decline breadth, limit-up/limit-down behavior, and whether leaders or laggards drove the move.
5. **Map the transmission chain**: write the hypothesized sequence, for example `surprise -> expected earnings or risk premium -> capital flow -> sector/fund NAV`. Mark each unsupported link as an inference.
6. **Red-team the story**: give the strongest alternative explanation, including crowded positioning, valuation, profit-taking, macro data, overseas markets, regulation, or simple index rebalancing.
7. **Build scenarios**: use base, bull, and bear cases with probability ranges, observable triggers, portfolio impact, and action. Probabilities must sum to approximately 100% and must be revised when new evidence arrives.
8. **Avoid event chasing**: an opening gap is not proof of persistence. For OTC funds, prefer evidence available before the 15:00 cutoff unless delay creates a clearly larger risk than the fee and NAV uncertainty.

## Evidence-quality score

Rate each decision High, Medium, or Low:

- **High**: authoritative event source, fresh market data, multiple confirming measures, known fees and trading status, and a clear invalidation test.
- **Medium**: verified event and fresh prices, but causal attribution or breadth confirmation remains mixed.
- **Low**: social-media-only claims, stale NAV, missing fees, conflicting sources, or a single-cause narrative. Low-quality evidence cannot authorize an action.

## Decision explanation contract

Explain an important decision in this order:

1. **事实**：what is verified, with timestamp and source.
2. **市场验证**：what prices, breadth, turnover, and relative strength actually did.
3. **因果链**：why the evidence may affect the fund, labeling inference as inference.
4. **反方观点**：the best competing explanation and what it predicts.
5. **情景树**：base, bull, bear probability ranges and observable triggers.
6. **行动**：amount, timing, fees, confirmation lag, and why this action dominates waiting.
7. **失效条件**：what evidence proves the thesis wrong and cancels or reverses the action.
8. **风险否决**：data, fee, status, concentration, QDII, drawdown, or holding-period vetoes.

## Teaching loop

Teach one reusable concept per important brief. Keep it short:

- Name the concept in plain language.
- Give the minimum formula or checklist needed to use it.
- Apply it to one current holding.
- State the common mistake it prevents.
- At the next review, compare the predicted observable evidence with what occurred.

Judge decision quality by whether the evidence, sizing, and invalidation discipline were sound—not by whether one trade happened to make money.
